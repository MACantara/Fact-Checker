üìñ Overview

Our fake news detection system uses a logistic regression machine learning model to analyze news articles and predict whether they are likely to be fake or real. The system is trained on the WELFake dataset, which contains over 72,000 labeled news articles.

The model combines natural language processing with logistic regression classification to achieve high accuracy in distinguishing between legitimate news and misinformation.

‚öôÔ∏è How It Works

1

Text Preprocessing

We clean and normalize the input text by removing URLs, HTML tags, special characters, and converting to lowercase. We then tokenize the text and remove common stop words.

2

Feature Extraction

We use TF-IDF (Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency) vectorization to convert text into numerical features that machine learning algorithms can understand. This captures the importance of words in the context of the entire dataset.

3

Logistic Regression Classification

Our trained logistic regression model analyzes the text features to make a prediction. This algorithm is well-suited for binary classification tasks like fake news detection, providing interpretable probability scores for both fake and real classifications.

4

Result Interpretation

The model outputs a probability score for both fake and real classifications. We provide confidence levels and interpretation to help users understand the reliability of the prediction.

‚ö†Ô∏è Limitations & Considerations

Not 100% Accurate: While our model achieves high accuracy, it's not perfect. AI systems can make mistakes, especially with nuanced or context-dependent content.

Training Data Bias: The model's predictions are influenced by the patterns it learned from the training data, which may not cover all types of misinformation.

Context Matters: The model analyzes text content but may miss important contextual factors like publication date, source credibility, or current events.

Evolving Misinformation: Fake news techniques constantly evolve, and the model may not catch the latest manipulation strategies.

Human Verification Needed: Always use critical thinking and verify information through multiple reliable sources, especially for important decisions.
